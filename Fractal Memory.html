The module is an 8x8 grid. Some cells will be colored red, green, blue, cyan, purple or yellow. Also, the borders of the cells of this grid can also be painted in one of the above colors, as well as in white or black. Each time any non-ignored module is solved, colored cells or cell borders may change.

Color Stage is valid if ...
Yellow odd starting time in minutes
Cyan exactly one battery holder
Green even number of indicators
Magenta 4th serial character > 4
Blue at least one PS/2 port
Red less than 29 modules

Refer to the table on the right. Using the color of the cell borders, determine the validity of the current stage. If stage is invalid then ignore the current stage.

Stage with white cell borders is always valid.

Stage with black cell borders is always invalid.

When all non-ignored modules have been solved, the entire grid will turn black and the module will be switched to submit mode. If there are no non-ignored modules on the bomb, then all cells will be initially colored: press any cell to switch to submit mode. The previously obtained information should be enough to determine the color of each grid cell. By pressing the cells you can change their colors. When eight different cells are given colors, pressing a black cell will be considered as a submit. The module will be solved if the resulting grid is the fractal described on the next page. Otherwise, you will get a strike and go into recovery mode.

When a strike is received, all cells will turn black, and the borders of the cells will turn red. Press up to eight cells to get their color. Pressing the ninth cell will exit recovery mode and go back to submit mode.
